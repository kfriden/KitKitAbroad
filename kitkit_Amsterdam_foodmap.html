KitKit's Travel Tips!

Transportation in the Netherlands can be a hassle at times, don't stress and get an OV chip card!

Getting an OV chipcard saves time so you can explore more! Simply purchase an anonymous chipcard (can find them being sold at airports, train stations, or newstands) and load your euros onto the card. Make sure at least 20 euros are on the card when using public transportation. You can find out more here

Must see places to visit in the Netherlands

Explore the best sites at Kinderdijk, a UNESCO heritage site, just outside of Rotterdam

Relax at Melief Bender and Biergarten, both with an impressive beer selection

Enjoy gorgeous paintings and art at the Rijksmuseum in the heart of Amsterdam

If you are a convention goer, the Jaarbeurs in Utrecht holds (one of my favorites) Dutch Comic Con every March and November. Get tickets here

American

Hard Rock Cafe Amsterdam: If you have been to any other Hard Rock Cafe, the menu is fairly the same just you are in Amsterdam. Great burgers, fries, and sandwiches.
Burger Meester: This place has an amazing selection of burgers to choose from, so make sure to come hungry! It's also very close to the ARTIS Zoo which I also recommend checking out.
Thrill Grill: Here you can feel good about what you eat as it's locally sourced and environmentally friendly. Thrill Grill has a little bit of everything to please everyone.
Bulls and Dogs: At Bulls and Dogs you can get hot dogs with a twist, and they even offer hotdogs paired with special beers. They also serve cocktails, sides, and a big selection of freakshakes.

Asian

La Mian: Not super extensive menu here but what they offer is outsanding! Perfect portions of noodles and noodle soups keeps bring people back for more.
Miss Korea BBQ: All you can eat Korean BBQ within 2.5 hours time. Choose from traditional delicious to eat or barbecue, prices are very reasonable here.
Izakaya Asian Kitchen and Bar: More on the expensive side, this Asian/Peruvian fusion restaurant offers so much, its often hard to choose. Incredible drink selection.
Tomo Sushi: Situated right near another great sushi place Shabu Shabu, Tomo Sushi has a great sushi and sashimi selection as well as 2 prix fix dinner options.

Dutch/Traditional

Haesje Claes: A local told me this is a great place to experience traditional Dutch food. Reasonably priced and great food and service. Make sure to make a reservation as this place can get pretty popular on weekends.
The Copper Kettle: Another great place if you are in search of trying traditional Dutch cuisine. One of the more fun parts is the tasting platters they offer.
De Fles: Fun little place located in one of the oldest cellars in Amsterdam. Serving up yummy fish and meat dishes served with salad and fries. I highly recommend the mussels for dinner!

Sweets/Desserts

Pancakehouse Upstairs: Dubbed the smallest restaurant in Europe, this pancake house is located in an old Amsterdam house dating back to 1539 with only 4 tables! Here you have a choice between different kinds of savory and sweet pancakes.
Melly's Cookies Bar: Great place to get anything from cookies, cakes, pastries, and sandwiches. Cozy place to sit and enjoy a cookie or piece of cake. Some say some of the best cookies they ever had.
The Pancake Bakery: If the border-line food porn video on the homepage of their website isn't an indication of how amazing their food must be then I don't know what is! This place has pretty much every Dutch pancake imaginable, savory and sweet. Also offers poffertjes (small pancakes), and omelets.

Seafood

Mr. Crab Bistro: For the fish/seafood lover this is the place to be. Great selection of burgers, tacos, soups, fish and chips, as well as small snacks.
Brut de Mer: Insanely fresh seafood with friendly service. I personally don't eat oysters, but I hear they are very good here.
The Good Company: Excellent fried fish snacks, especially the shrimp croquettes. This place also makes fresh fish and chips daily and focuses on fresh local products and sustainable fish!
