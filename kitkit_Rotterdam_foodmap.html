KitKit's Travel Tips!

Transportation in the Netherlands can be a hassle at times, don't stress and get an OV chip card!

Getting an OV chipcard saves time so you can explore more! Simply purchase an anonymous chipcard (can find them being sold at airports, train stations, or newstands) and load your euros onto the card. Make sure at least 20 euros are on the card when using public transportation. You can find out more here

Must see places to visit in the Netherlands

Explore the best sites at Kinderdijk, a UNESCO heritage site, just outside of Rotterdam

Relax at Melief Bender and Biergarten, both with an impressive beer selection

Enjoy gorgeous paintings and art at the Rijksmuseum in the heart of Amsterdam

If you are a convention goer, the Jaarbeurs in Utrecht holds (one of my favorites) Dutch Comic Con every March and November. Get tickets here

Asian

Shabu Shabu: Just like Miu in Amsterdam, this is an all you can eat style Japanese/sushi restaurant. My absolute favorite when I am in Rotterdam.
Takumi Dusseldorf Netherlands: Feeling in the mood for ramen? This place has you covered! The portions here are big so you will leave with a full belly of noodles. I recommend the tonkotsu ramen, can't go wrong with it :)
China Town Dim Sum Rotterdam: Fun little place you can go for dim sum. Most of the items are fairly reasonably priced, good for sharing with a group of people.
Grand Garden Restaurant: Great place for that authentic Asian cuisine. Located inside the NH Hotel Rotterdam.
Seoul Sista: All you can eat Korean BBQ with a great variety and selection of foods.

American

Gracy's: American style 50's diner in the heart of Rotterdam. If you are in the mood for burgers this place is a must try!
Encore Bar and Grill: Here you can customize your dinner from your choice of meat/fish or burgers with sides and a salad. Great service and drink selections.
Rotown: A lovely bar/restaurant. They serve wonderful food as a restaurant and as a bar frequently have live music of all sorts. So whether you go for the food or the music, this is a great place to visit.

Dutch/Traditional

Old Dutch: On the more expensive side, Old Dutch features classics with a fancy flare. They also feature a fun selection of drinks as well as prix fix courses that change daily.
Prachtig: Set right beside the well known Erasmusbrug on the water, this restaurant not only gives a picturesque view, but also gives a great selection of traditional fare to choose from.

Sweets/Desserts

Poffertjessalon Seth: When in Holland, poffertjes are a must have! These tiny pancakes are served with icing sugar and your choice of fruit.
Bakers Dough: Craving cookie dough and ice cream? This place serves raw (but safe to eat) cookie dough with your choice of fruits and/or ice cream.
Roll'd Up Ice Cream: Watch in awe as your favorite ice cream is made in front of you. Pick your base, mix-ins, and toppings to create your own masterpiece! There is no website yet, but they are located close to the Market Hall.

Seafood

The Fish Market: A reasonably affordable place to get some of the best fish around! Definitely give this place a try.
Zeezout: More on the expensive side for seafood, Zeezout is praised as being one of the best fish restaurants in South Holland. If your budget allows, this is a place that can't be beat.
